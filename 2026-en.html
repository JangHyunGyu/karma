What year is 2026 in Korean astrology?

In Korean astrology, 2026 is Byeong-O year (丙午) — the 43rd of the 60-year sexagenary cycle. The heavenly stem 'Byeong (丙)' represents Yang Fire — like the blazing sun. The earthly branch 'O (午)' represents the red horse. Together they form the "Year of the Red Horse" (적마년 / 赤馬年).

Because both pillars carry Fire energy, Byeong-O is considered an exceptionally strong Fire year. In East Asian metaphysics, such years are seen as times of transformation, passion, creativity, and bold expression. It's a great year to start something new — but the excess Fire also brings risks: conflict, burnout, and impulsive decisions.

Historically, Byeong-O years have coincided with major political and cultural shifts. The 1906 Byeong-O year fell during the turbulent late Korean Empire, and the 1966 Byeong-O year saw Korea enter its industrial growth era. 2026 is similarly read as a potential turning-point year for both individuals and society.

Frequently asked questions

Q. When does the 2026 Year of the Red Horse officially begin?

In Korean Saju, the year begins at Ipchun (立春), around February 4, 2026 in the solar calendar — not at Lunar New Year. From this date, the Byeong-O Fire energy fully takes effect.

Q. Is being a Horse in your Self Year (本命年) bad?

Not necessarily. The Self Year means major changes — not necessarily misfortune. Depending on your full birth chart and current major fortune cycle (大運), it can actually be a transformative breakthrough year.
